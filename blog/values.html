CORE WEEK 1: Identity, values and strengths

What is a situation where I was faced with an ethical dilemma?


How have the people around me helped shape my values?

Like a lot of children growing up I took a lot of notice of my parents and my auntie (she visited once a year so I have spent a lot of time with her), how they acted, their values, and the way they conducted themselves. My parents are both extremely honest, hard working and genuine people and naturally I feel like a lot of those values have rubbed off on me and helped shaped me into the person I am today, and the values that I follow and hold close to me.


What is a strength of mine and how will it help me on my learning journey?

I think one of my biggest strengths that will help me on my learning journey, and especially as a programmer, is that I am good at thinking outside of the box, looking at problems and finding a solution, and thinking of different ways to solve the problems - or on the other hand, thinking of different ways to achieve a desired result.


What are my limitations in terms of learning and career?

I think a big limitation of mine that may affect my learning and career aspirations is my struggle to focus. I have for as long as I can remember struggled to stay focus on a single task for more than 10-15 minutes at a time. My brain just jumps from one idea or project to another so one thing you may notice is that I will constantly have 3 or 4 different projects going at the exact same time, because my brain just gets bored of doing one task and then switches off and jumps to another task.


Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

Back in college I was put in a group project with a few other students, one of which I didn’t get on well with at all and frankly I didn’t like - this person felt the same towards me so there was quite a bit of tension. Ultimately, I recognised that this group project was important towards my final grade and decided to put my dislike for this person aside and try work effectively with them to try and do the best possible work. How effective was this? Well I still found this person unbelievably annoying through-out the entire project timeline, but we got a great mark so I guess it worked?

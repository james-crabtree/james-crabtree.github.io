Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because having those core skills that we are being taught is something that a lot of employers in the programming/technology industry looks for in potential candiates to hire. Real world technology employers aren't just looking for people who can code well, with enough time and practice basically anyone can code well, they're also looking for people who can work efficiently, work well in a team and have good time management skills. This is why Dev Academy teaches us these core skills, to get us ready for work in the real world and to also make us look more attractive to potential employers.


Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, absolutely not. I suppose a lot of people just think that working as a programmer is all about writing code, and while that is pretty true, there is a whole lot more to it than that, such as problem solving, working to strict deadlines, working in a team, and keeping your cool when shit just isn’t going right. This is where the core learning comes in, knowing how to program is essential as a programmer but so are the other core things so I certainly don’t think it is a waste of time to be studying the core learnings.
